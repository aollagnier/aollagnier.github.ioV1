Anaïs Ollagnier, PhD

Post-Doctorant at Inria

Short Bio.

Since January 2021, I am a post-doctorant fellow in computer science at the I3S Laboratory where I am a member of the joint Inria-CNRS-UCA Wimmics team led by Fabien Gandon and based in Sophia Antipolis, ca. Nice (France).

My research area is Artificial Intelligence (AI), and my current work focuses on identifying and preventing cyberbullying on social media. I am particularly interested in data mining aiming at developing innovative techniques to improve community detection and counter-argument generation.

In November 2017 I received the Ph.D. in computer science from the Aix-Marseille Université (France), with a thesis focused on developing a content-based recommender system using natural language processing techniques. I conducted my PhD research in the Data, Information content MAnagement Group (DIMAG) in the LIS (Computer and Systems Laboratory) at Marseille (France). As a part of my work, I also had the opportunity to collaborate with one of the world's leading platforms in electronic publishing in the field of Humanities and Social Sciences (HSS): OpenEdition. My contribution to BILBO (Automatic reference labelling) resulted in a publicly available tool which is still one of the main components of digital tools developed in the OpenEdition Lab.

In 2018, I moved to the University of Exeter (UK) for a two-year postdoc in the Social & Environmental Data Analysis (SEDA) lab to work on a career coach for personalised learning. This work resulted in a fully integrated self-directed career exploration tool called C3-IoC whose a publicly version is available online.

Research interests: NLP, Machine Learning, Information Extration, Data Mining.
